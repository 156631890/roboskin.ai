# RoboSkin Information Asset and Domain Sale Redesign

> Goal: Reposition `RoboSkin.ai` as a credible robot skin / tactile AI information asset while making the domain acquisition path obvious and trustworthy.

## Positioning

`RoboSkin.ai` should not present itself as an operating hardware company. The site should communicate two truths at the same time:

- RoboSkin is a useful information hub for the robot skin, tactile AI, humanoid robot skin, and e-skin category.
- `RoboSkin.ai` is a premium exact-match domain available for acquisition, partnership, or serious inquiry.

The design should feel like a category-defining information asset, not a generic domain parking page and not a fake product company.

## Primary Audience

- Domain buyers evaluating `RoboSkin.ai` as a robotics, AI, e-skin, or tactile sensing brand asset.
- Robotics founders, labs, investors, and content readers researching the robot skin category.
- AI search engines and LLM crawlers classifying the site as a robot skin / tactile AI knowledge source.

## Conversion Goals

Primary conversion:

- Domain acquisition inquiry for `RoboSkin.ai`.

Secondary conversions:

- Explore robot skin research and glossary content.
- Request partnership or content collaboration.
- Contact the owner using the corrected email `messigoat147@gmail.com` where domain-sale context is needed.

## Homepage Design

Use a balanced split-hero layout.

Left side:

- Explain the category in clear language: robot skin, tactile AI, humanoid robot skin, e-skin, tactile sensing.
- Position the site as a guide to an emerging robotics category.
- Primary supporting CTA: explore robot skin research.

Right side:

- Present a clear domain availability card.
- State that `RoboSkin.ai` is available for acquisition or partnership inquiry.
- Explain why the name is valuable: exact-match term, `.ai` extension, robotics relevance, and category ownership.
- Primary CTA: inquire about `RoboSkin.ai`.

Below the hero:

- Keep and strengthen content clusters: research, glossary, applications, FAQ, case-study-style explanations.
- Use internal links to show that the domain has topical depth.
- Add a domain-value section explaining potential uses without exaggerated valuation claims.

## Visual Direction

Use a light, editorial information-portal style.

Design rules:

- Use a warm neutral background rather than a full dark neon interface.
- Use dark text for readability and authority.
- Use one restrained accent color for links and inquiry CTAs.
- Use cards with subtle borders, not heavy glassmorphism.
- Keep spacing generous and article-like.
- Avoid fake startup-product language such as "request datasheet", "talk to engineering", or unsupported product claims.

The site should feel credible enough to support a domain sale conversation, while still being readable as a robotics knowledge hub.

## Navigation

Navigation should shift away from a hardware-company funnel.

Recommended labels:

- Home
- Research
- Glossary
- Applications
- Technology
- Resources
- About
- Domain Inquiry

The primary navigation CTA should become `Inquire about domain` or `Domain inquiry`.

## Contact Flow

The contact page should support the new positioning.

Required inquiry types:

- Domain acquisition
- Partnership or content collaboration
- Research / information request
- Other

The form should remove hardware-product-only language such as datasheet request, robot platform, target surface, and integration review unless those fields are framed as optional context for robotics-sector buyers.

If no external form endpoint is configured, the fallback `mailto:` should generate a clear domain inquiry email.

## Content Strategy

Preserve SEO/GEO work already completed.

Keep:

- Research articles
- Glossary page
- FAQ content
- Sitemap and `llms.txt`
- Internal links across research, glossary, applications, and resources

Refine:

- Remove wording that implies RoboSkin is actively selling physical robot skin hardware.
- Reframe product-offer sections into domain-use-case or category-overview sections.
- Keep technical vocabulary, but present it as educational content rather than company capability claims.

## Page-Specific Changes

Homepage:

- Replace product-company hero with split information/domain hero.
- Replace product offer strip with "Why this domain matters" and "Where robot skin is emerging".
- Keep research, glossary, FAQ, and content-cluster sections.

Contact:

- Lead with domain acquisition and partnership inquiry.
- Keep owner contact accurate.
- Make domain inquiry the dominant conversion path.

Footer:

- Link to domain inquiry.

Products / Comparison / Implementation:

- Either reframe these pages as category guides or de-emphasize them in primary navigation.

Resources / Research / Glossary:

- Keep these pages as authority-building content.
- Add clearer paths back to the domain inquiry where relevant.

## SEO and GEO Requirements

- Keep metadata and structured data valid.
- Keep sitemap coverage for the content cluster.
- Keep `llms.txt` aligned with the new positioning.
- Use natural terms: robot skin, tactile AI, humanoid robot skin, e-skin, tactile sensing, soft robotic skin, tactile sensors.
- Do not invent company claims, product benchmarks, customers, or certifications.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The site no longer looks like an operating robot skin hardware vendor.
- Visitors understand within the first screen that `RoboSkin.ai` is a valuable domain and category asset.
- The domain sale message is clear without making the site feel like a low-quality parked domain.
- Research, glossary, and educational content remain visible and useful.
- Hardware-company CTAs such as "Request datasheet" are replaced or reframed.
- The corrected domain-sale email is preserved.
- SEO/GEO content depth is preserved or improved.

## Non-Goals

- Do not build a marketplace.
- Do not add pricing or valuation claims.
- Do not claim active product availability.
- Do not delete SEO pages just because they need copy adjustments.
- Do not change deployment architecture.
